Research Strategy Finder
Hattie: Feedback (d=0.73)
Hattie
One of the highest-impact interventions. Timely, specific feedback on the task — not the person — dramatically increases student achievement. For teachers: model giving feedback in lesson debrief.
TLAC: Cold Call (Technique 22)
TLAC
Call on students regardless of hand-raising. Normalizes participation for all students and maintains academic accountability throughout independent and whole-group work. Maps to MO Standard 5.2.
Marzano: Cooperative Learning (Element 25)
Marzano
Structured group work with individual accountability increases engagement and content mastery. Map to MO Standards 4.3 and 5.2. Pair with an accountability artifact — exit slip, sticky note, turn-and-talk.

Data Protection
Privacy & Zero Data Retention
Built for zero liability — not by policy, but by architecture. Here's exactly what that means.
🎓 FERPA
FERPA is triggered when student education records are stored or transmitted to a third party persistently. This tool never stores a student record. Uploading a gradebook PDF here is functionally identical to opening it in your browser — FERPA does not regulate that. No student name, ID, or score ever touches a server in a stored state.
👩‍🏫 MNEA / MSTA Union
Teacher unions have concerns about observation data in district-controlled systems that can be subpoenaed. None of that applies here. Observation notes, eval progress, and coaching records exist only in your session. There is no district-controlled database. There is nothing to subpoena. This tool is a smart notepad — not a surveillance system.
⚖️ HR Exposure
HR issues arise when informal notes about employees exist in accessible systems. Because nothing is stored between sessions, there is no repository of draft observations or coaching notes that anyone else could access — not your principal, not HR, not a district administrator. Your notes are yours until you choose to formally transmit them through official channels.
☁️ What the AI Sees
When you request AI-generated content, your text is sent to Claude via an encrypted Cloudflare Worker. Claude processes it and returns a response. The Worker does not log, store, or transmit content elsewhere. Use role-based labels ("3rd Grade ELA teacher") rather than real names in AI prompts.
💻 Personal Device
Use this tool on a personal device on personal wifi — not a district-owned device on the school network. District IT monitoring can see network traffic on school equipment. Since this is your personal tool at your personal URL, using it on a personal device is the natural use case.
🏫 Changing Schools
Because nothing from any school is ever stored, no data carries over when you move. When you update School Setup, you only change a display name and colors. No student records, eval history, or coaching notes transfer — because none of it was ever retained. Clean slate every time.
30-second explanation for administrators:
"This tool is a browser-based smart notepad. It helps me analyze data and draft documents, but it stores nothing. The moment I close the tab, everything is gone — no database, no server history, no retained records. It's the same as using a PDF reader — nothing persists unless I explicitly save and send it through official channels."

How the Proficiency Analyzer Works

1. Export from Infinite Campus
In IC, go to your gradebook → Reports → Grade Export. Export as PDF, CSV, or Excel.

2. Upload Here
Drop the file in the upload zone. The tool reads it entirely in your browser — no data leaves your device until you optionally request AI analysis.

3. Standard Detection
The tool looks for Missouri Learning Standard codes (RL.1-3, OA, K.PS1.A, etc.) in the assignment names and maps them to proficiency levels 1–4.

4. AI Analysis (optional)
If you've set up your Worker URL in School Setup, you can request an AI-generated gap analysis. No student names are sent — only anonymous score distributions.

Zero Data Retained
When you close this tab, all uploaded data is gone. Nothing is logged, stored, or transmitted without your explicit action.
